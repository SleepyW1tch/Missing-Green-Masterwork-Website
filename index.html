The Problem

Why does misinformation thrive in today's digital space? Younger generations are exposed to vast amounts of information daily, yet they often lack the critical tools to discern truth from manipulation. Education plays a crucial role in shaping conscious consumers of online content.

Sustainability and climate issues are particularly vulnerable to greenwashing and deceptive narratives. This project aims to combat this challenge through an engaging, educational game.

Read about it in my thesis

Project Description

The aim of this masterwork project is to passively educate young people (14+), mostly high school students and adults on the topics and tipical behaviours of greenwashing.

The project consists of a shorter hybrid game, using both physical cards and the game application on their phones. They take on the role of representing the Brand persona (CEO) and the marketing personas. The marketing players have to compete for money by doing the best pitch to save the brand/company from specific accusations. Tricky actions, like attacking takes place in the app.

Classroom Testing

Initial classroom test sessions have provided valuable insights. Students engaged actively, discussed real-life cases, and refined their ability to spot greenwashing tactics.

  • Session 1: Understanding misinformation through real-world examples.
  • Session 2: Role-playing scenarios based on common greenwashing techniques.
  • Session 3: Debriefing and discussing improvements.

Highlighted Rules

  • The player who gets the most rewarded with money wins!
  • You have 2mins to prepare your pitch.
  • If there are many people playing, you should create groups (5+), otherwise stick to the individual players setting.
  • You have to incorporate at least 2 words from the pitch deck.
  • You are not allowed to use your phone for anything else other than what is in the app.
