The Problem

Why does misinformation thrive in today's digital space? Younger generations are exposed to vast amounts of information daily, yet they often lack the critical tools to discern truth from manipulation. Education plays a crucial role in shaping conscious consumers of online content.

Sustainability and climate issues are particularly vulnerable to greenwashing and deceptive narratives. This project aims to combat this challenge through an engaging, educational game.

Read more about it in my thesis

Project Description

The aim of this masterwork project is to teach young people (13+), mostly high school students, about sustainability—more specifically, greenwashing.

The project consists of a 45-minute card-based game where players take on different personas, each with unique abilities and goals. Players must navigate social media scenarios, work together, and strategize to achieve their objectives while identifying misinformation.

Classroom Testing

Initial classroom test sessions have provided valuable insights. Students engaged actively, discussed real-life cases, and refined their ability to spot greenwashing tactics.

  • Session 1: Understanding misinformation through real-world examples.
  • Session 2: Role-playing scenarios based on common greenwashing techniques.
  • Session 3: Debriefing and discussing improvements.
